This SpongeBob SquarePants meme is taking over the internet, but wHaT DoEs iT mEaN?

Share this article

Alysha Tsuji
like
May 12, 2017 6:16 pm

Have you seen that meme of SpongeBob all bent over with his face looking weird with people typing captions that LoOk liKE tHiS? Are you wondering what the heck it means? For The Win is here to help.

Where did it come from?

Now known best as the Mocking SpongeBob Meme, it has become extremely popular this month. The screengrab comes from an episode that aired on Nov. 25, 2012. Here’s the clip:

In case anyone was wondering where that "spongebob mocking" meme came from..😂 pic.twitter.com/50BJhCsFY0

— Sutton 💁🏽‍♂️ (@suttonimpaQt) May 11, 2017

According to the brilliant site knowyourmeme.com, there were two of the first most popular tweets that turned the screengrab from that episode into jokes:

How i stare back at little kids when they stare for too long pic.twitter.com/do0XjMPP19

— dennis. (@OGBEARD) May 5, 2017

Bf: "I don't even know her like that"
Me: "I doNt EveN KnOw heR liKe thAt" pic.twitter.com/xj4Fi5j2h1

— lexy🌹 (@lexysaeyang) May 5, 2017

But what does it mean?

It’s just a way to mock something. That’s it. All you do is write something that you think deserves to be heard in a mocking tone.

A sports example is this tweet trolling “James Harden for MVP” supporters after he had an awful performance in Game 6:

jAmEs HaRdEn Is ThE mVp pic.twitter.com/O96W2itr3V

— Dylan (@DylansFreshTake) May 12, 2017

Other examples include this:

iPhone users: "Android quality is trash"

Android users: "aNdRoId QwUliaTY iS TrwAsh" pic.twitter.com/Pf8vBfVU2j

— ㅤ (@JayNoHoes) May 8, 2017

Or this:

mcdonald's: "our ice cream machine broke"
me: "oUR eyeZ kreeM muhSheen broKe" pic.twitter.com/8UY4aOzo40

— ⚡️blackstepfather ⚡️ (@zephaniiiah) May 8, 2017

OK, but I still don’t get it. It’s not funny.

If you’re still confused, I’m sorry. Like with most memes, there really isn’t anything to get. There is no secret meaning in which letters are capitalized or how things are spelled. It’s just funny if you look at the screengrab and imagine Spongebob screeching about whatever the caption is.

If you really hate it, don’t worry, a new meme will take over soon.

"Dont use that weird spongebob mocking meme"

Me: DonT uSe thAt WeIrd SpoNgEboB MoCkinG MEme pic.twitter.com/ompUk9su7n

— spongemock (@TheSpongeMock) May 10, 2017

SpongeBob forever.
